Recorded testimonies of military leaders, freedom fighters, and civilians documenting personal experiences of the 1971 Liberation War.

M. A. G. Osmani

Role: Commander-in-Chief, Mukti Bahini
Birth Year: 1918
Interview Date: 1972

Biography: General Osmani served as the supreme commander of Bangladesh Liberation Forces, leading strategic military planning during the war.

Interview Summary: He explains military coordination, international support, and operational challenges faced during the Liberation War.

K. M. Shafiullah

Role: Sector Commander (Sector 3)
Birth Year: 1934
Interview Date: 2010

Biography: A key sector commander who organized resistance fighters and guerrilla warfare in eastern regions.

Interview Summary: He describes battlefield leadership, training, and coordination among sectors.

Abu Taher

Role: Freedom Fighter & Military Officer
Birth Year: 1938
Interview Date: 2008

Biography: An active guerrilla commander who motivated fighters during intense combat phases.

Interview Summary: He reflects on ideological motivation, sacrifices, and armed resistance.

Ziaur Rahman

Role: Sector Commander
Birth Year: 1936
Interview Date: 1974

Biography: Prominent military leader known for broadcasting the declaration of independence.

Interview Summary: He emphasizes leadership, unity, and morale during wartime operations.

Mohammad Abdul Jalil

Role: Sector Commander (Sector 9)
Birth Year: 1940
Interview Date: 2011

Biography: Led naval and river-based operations in southern Bangladesh.

Interview Summary: He discusses river warfare strategy and coastal resistance.

Mohammad Ali

Role: Civilian Survivor
Birth Year: 1952
Interview Date: 2015

Biography: Witnessed displacement, military operations, and refugee movements as a teenager.

Interview Summary: He shares experiences of civilian suffering and long-term psychological effects.
