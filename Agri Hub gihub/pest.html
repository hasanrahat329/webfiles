Pest Control

Effective pest control methods to protect crops, improve yield and ensure sustainable agriculture.

Aphid Control

Aphids suck plant sap and reduce crop growth. Neem oil, insecticidal soap and lady beetles are effective.

Caterpillar Control

Caterpillars damage leaves. BT pesticide and hand removal help control them.

Spider Mite

Causes leaf yellowing. Control with regular watering and miticides.

Fruit Fly Control

Fruit flies damage fruits internally. Bait traps and proper sanitation are effective.

Locust Management

Locusts cause massive crop loss. Early detection, spraying and biological control are essential.

Termite Control

Termites damage roots and wood. Soil treatment is the most effective method.

Armyworm Control

Sudden crop damage can be reduced using pheromone traps.

Weevil Control

Weevils are destructive pests that mainly attack stored grains, seeds, and some field crops by boring holes and feeding from inside, which reduces both quality and yield. Effective weevil control starts with proper sanitation, such as cleaning storage areas and removing infested grains. Using airtight containers and maintaining low moisture levels helps prevent their spread. Regular inspection, pheromone traps, and safe insecticide application can significantly reduce weevil populations. Integrated Pest Management (IPM), combining preventive storage practices and biological or chemical control methods, is the most sustainable approach to managing weevil infestations.
