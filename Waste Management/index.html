Hazardous Waste

Hazardous waste refers to materials that can be harmful to human health or the environment if not properly managed. These wastes include chemicals, medical waste, batteries, paints, and industrial by-products. Improper disposal of hazardous waste can lead to soil contamination, water pollution, and air quality issues, posing serious risks to both humans and wildlife. Effective management involves safe collection, treatment, recycling, and disposal, along with public awareness and strict regulatory policies. Waste to Energy

It is the process of converting non-recyclable waste materials into usable forms of energy, such as electricity, heat, or fuel. This approach helps reduce the volume of waste sent to landfills while generating renewable energy, contributing to environmental sustainability. Technologies like incineration, anaerobic digestion, and gasification are commonly used to extract energy safely from waste. Implementing waste-to-energy systems not only decreases landfill dependency but also provides an eco-friendly solution to meet growing energy demands.
